## ðŸ§® Manual Calculation Formula

### Future Value of Annuity (Regular Contributions)

```
FV = PMT Ã— [(1 + r)^n - 1] / r

Where:
FV  = Future Value
PMT = Payment per period (annual contribution)
r   = Annual interest rate (as decimal)
n   = Number of periods (years)
```

**Example:**
- Annual contribution: $7,000
- Rate: 8% (0.08)
- Years: 30

```
FV = 7,000 Ã— [(1.08)^30 - 1] / 0.08
FV = 7,000 Ã— [10.0627 - 1] / 0.08
FV = 7,000 Ã— 9.0627 / 0.08
FV = 7,000 Ã— 113.28
FV = $792,960
```

## ðŸŽ“ Rule of 72

**Quick way to calculate doubling time:**

```
Years to double = 72 / Interest Rate

Examples:
- 6% return: 72 / 6 = 12 years
- 7% return: 72 / 7 = 10.3 years
- 8% return: 72 / 8 = 9 years
- 10% return: 72 / 10 = 7.2 years
```

**What this means for you:**
- $100K at 8% becomes $200K in 9 years
- $200K becomes $400K in another 9 years
- $400K becomes $800K in another 9 years

**From age 25-52 (27 years = 3 doublings):**
- $100K â†’ $200K â†’ $400K â†’ $800K
- Plus all your new contributions!
