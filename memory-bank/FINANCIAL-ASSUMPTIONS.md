### Mega Backdoor Roth (Advanced Strategy)
- **What it is:** After-tax 401(k) contributions converted to Roth
- **Total 401(k) Limit:** $70,000/year (employee + employer contributions)
- **Potential Mega Backdoor Amount:** Up to $44,100/year (after maxing regular 401(k) and employer match)
- **Requirements:** 
  - Employer plan allows after-tax contributions
  - Employer plan allows in-service conversions
  - Must max regular 401(k) first ($23,500)
- **Known Supporting Employers:** Amazon, Google, Microsoft, Meta, Apple, and other large tech companies
- **Tax Benefit:** Contributions are after-tax, but all growth is tax-free (like Roth IRA)
- **Income Limits:** None (works at any income level)
- **Priority:** Only after maxing 401(k) ($23,500) and Roth IRA ($7,000)

**Example Calculation (for someone earning $150K+):**
```
Total 401(k) limit:                     $70,000
Regular 401(k) contribution:            -$23,500
Employer match (3% of $150K):           -$4,500
Available for Mega Backdoor Roth:       $42,000
```

**30-Year Impact at 8% return:**
- Mega Backdoor ($42,000/year): $5.2 million tax-free
- Same in taxable account: $4.1 million (after 20% capital gains)
- **Tax savings: $1.1 million**
